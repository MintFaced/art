Two Burdens — Painting — 2024
Biography

MintFace works across painting, photography, generative code, and artificial intelligence — using each medium as a different pressure applied to the same question: what remains after you subtract the unnecessary?

Based in New Zealand, MintFace was among the early artists to tokenize work on the Ethereum blockchain, establishing a practice where ownership, provenance, and physical presence coexist without hierarchy. Collectors in Spain, Belgium, Switzerland, Australia and the United States hold paintings that began as NFTs and arrived, eventually, as objects on walls.

The work is deeply personal — structured around a father's desire to leave a trail of evidence: paintings, geodetic coordinates, metadata, inscriptions on Bitcoin. Things that might one day be found.

Patrimora

Generative Art — On-chain

Inspired by senbazuru — the Japanese tradition of folding 1,000 origami cranes. Named for a word MintFace coined: patrimora — a legacy delayed in its realization. A memorial to a son growing up without a father, threaded through the motif of cranes, thought to represent long life.

Two
Burdens

Oil on Canvas — 36 works

The inner and outer burdens we all carry. The surface is worked over and over — adding, subtracting, adding, subtracting — until an intensity presents itself. Influenced by Richter's later work, but where Richter sought to remove the human hand, here the mindset is active, not indifferent. The intermediation between thought and paint is everything.

Acquired by collectors in Spain, Belgium, Switzerland, Australia and the USA. Exhibited at NFT NYC 2024 and The Line, New Zealand.

Recursive
Mind

Illustration — Photoshop — Bitcoin Ordinals

Endless thought patterns looping to infinity. Pixels pushed until they break. What remains becomes the art — a study in deletion, in removal, in sitting with what is left after pressure subsides.

No AI. All hand-drawn in Photoshop — a tool used almost without interruption since age 13. The works sat in a folder for two years before being inscribed as Ordinals on Bitcoin: an extraordinarily expensive act that binds their longevity to Bitcoin's longevity.

Roads
& Rivers

Photography — Aerial — North Island, NZ

Roads are for travelling. Rivers go with the flow. When the two overlap, you get to decide which way you go. MintFace drove the North Island of New Zealand — post-pandemic, little traffic, the freedom of open roads — photographing bridges.

Seven of the 36 bridges were washed away in Cyclone Gabrielle, February 2023. The collection sparked conversations about memory, crossing, where people were going and why it mattered. Featured in the NZ Herald.
